## Overview

This document provides guidelines for student presentations as part of the AI Diploma program assessment methods.

---

## 1. Presentation Requirements | متطلبات العرض التقديمي

### 1.1 Types of Presentations

**Case Study Presentations:**
- Analyze a real-world problem
- Present solution approach
- Discuss findings and recommendations
- Duration: 15-20 minutes + 5 minutes Q&A

**Project Presentations:**
- Present capstone project
- Demonstrate working solution
- Show results and evaluation
- Duration: 20-30 minutes + 10 minutes Q&A

**Technical Presentations:**
- Explain a concept or technique
- Demonstrate implementation
- Show practical applications
- Duration: 10-15 minutes + 5 minutes Q&A

---

## 2. Presentation Structure | هيكل العرض التقديمي

### 2.1 Standard Structure

**Introduction (2-3 minutes)**
- Title and presenter name
- Problem statement or topic overview
- Objectives and scope
- Outline of presentation

**Main Content (10-20 minutes)**
- Background and context
- Methodology or approach
- Implementation or analysis
- Results and findings
- Discussion and insights

**Conclusion (2-3 minutes)**
- Summary of key points
- Main takeaways
- Future work or recommendations
- Questions and discussion

---

## 3. Content Guidelines | إرشادات المحتوى

### 3.1 Visual Design

**Slides:**
- Clear, readable fonts (minimum 24pt)
- Consistent color scheme
- Professional design
- Limited text per slide (6-8 bullet points max)
- Use visuals: diagrams, charts, code snippets

**Best Practices:**
- One main idea per slide
- Use high-quality images
- Ensure good contrast
- Test readability from distance

### 3.2 Technical Content

**Code Demonstrations:**
- Show key code snippets
- Explain logic and approach
- Highlight important parts
- Use syntax highlighting
- Keep snippets concise

**Results Presentation:**
- Use charts and graphs
- Show before/after comparisons
- Include metrics and statistics
- Explain what results mean
- Discuss limitations

---

## 4. Delivery Guidelines | إرشادات العرض

### 4.1 Speaking Skills

**Voice:**
- Speak clearly and at appropriate pace
- Vary tone and emphasis
- Pause for emphasis
- Avoid filler words ("um", "uh")

**Body Language:**
- Maintain eye contact
- Use gestures appropriately
- Stand confidently
- Move naturally (don't hide behind podium)

### 4.2 Time Management

**Preparation:**
- Practice your presentation
- Time each section
- Prepare for questions
- Have backup slides for deep dives

**During Presentation:**
- Stay within time limit
- Monitor audience engagement
- Adjust pace if needed
- Save time for Q&A

## 5. Evaluation Rubric | معايير التقييم

### 5.1 Content (40 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (36-40) | Good (28-35) | Satisfactory (24-27) | Needs Improvement (<24) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Clarity | Very clear, well-organized | Clear, mostly organized | Somewhat clear | Unclear or confusing |
| Depth | Comprehensive coverage | Good coverage | Basic coverage | Superficial |
| Accuracy | All information accurate | Mostly accurate | Some errors | Significant errors |
| Relevance | Highly relevant | Relevant | Somewhat relevant | Off-topic |

### 5.2 Delivery (30 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (27-30) | Good (21-26) | Satisfactory (18-20) | Needs Improvement (<18) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Clarity | Very clear speech | Clear speech | Mostly clear | Hard to understand |
| Engagement | Highly engaging | Engaging | Somewhat engaging | Not engaging |
| Pace | Perfect pace | Good pace | Too fast/slow | Poor pace |
| Confidence | Very confident | Confident | Somewhat confident | Lacks confidence |

### 5.3 Visuals (20 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (18-20) | Good (14-17) | Satisfactory (12-13) | Needs Improvement (<12) |
|----------|-------------------|--------------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Design | Professional, polished | Good design | Basic design | Poor design |
| Clarity | Very clear visuals | Clear visuals | Somewhat clear | Unclear |
| Relevance | Highly relevant | Relevant | Somewhat relevant | Irrelevant |
| Quality | High quality | Good quality | Acceptable | Poor quality |

### 5.4 Q&A (10 points)

| Criteria | Excellent (9-10) | Good (7-8) | Satisfactory (6) | Needs Improvement (<6) |
|----------|-----------------|------------|------------------|------------------------|
| Understanding | Deep understanding | Good understanding | Basic understanding | Poor understanding |
| Responses | Clear, accurate | Mostly clear | Somewhat clear | Unclear or incorrect |

**Total: 100 points**

## 7. Common Mistakes to Avoid | أخطاء شائعة يجب تجنبها

### 7.1 Content Mistakes

- ❌ Too much text on slides
- ❌ Reading directly from slides
- ❌ Not explaining technical terms
- ❌ Skipping important details
- ❌ No clear structure

### 7.2 Delivery Mistakes

- ❌ Speaking too fast
- ❌ Not making eye contact
- ❌ Apologizing excessively
- ❌ Going over time limit
- ❌ Not preparing for questions

### 7.3 Visual Mistakes

- ❌ Poor color contrast
- ❌ Too many animations
- ❌ Low-quality images
- ❌ Inconsistent formatting
- ❌ Cluttered slides

## 9. Tips for Success | نصائح للنجاح

### 9.1 Preparation Tips

1. **Practice:** Rehearse multiple times
2. **Record:** Record yourself to identify improvements
3. **Feedback:** Get feedback from peers
4. **Timing:** Practice with timer
5. **Questions:** Prepare for likely questions

### 9.2 Delivery Tips

1. **Start Strong:** Engaging opening
2. **Tell a Story:** Make it narrative
3. **Use Examples:** Real-world examples
4. **Engage Audience:** Ask questions, use polls
5. **End Memorable:** Strong conclusion

### 9.3 Technical Tips

1. **Test Equipment:** Before presentation
2. **Have Backup:** Multiple formats
3. **Internet:** Don't rely on live demos requiring internet
4. **Fonts:** Embed fonts or use common ones
5. **Videos:** Test video playback
